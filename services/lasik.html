LASIK

Custom Bladeless LASIK

LASIK (Laser-Assisted In Situ Keratomileusis) is the world’s most performed elective surgical procedure — with over 700,000 procedures performed in the US each year and a patient satisfaction rate consistently above 96%. During LASIK, a femtosecond laser creates a thin flap in the cornea. An excimer laser then precisely reshapes the underlying corneal tissue to correct your refractive error — nearsightedness, farsightedness, or astigmatism. The flap is repositioned and heals naturally, with no stitches required. The procedure takes about 10 minutes per eye. Most patients see 20/20 or better by the next morning.

Bladeless Technique

100% laser procedure — no blade used at any point. Maximum safety and precision.

Custom Wavefront Treatment

We map your eye’s unique optical fingerprint to create a truly personalized correction.

Rapid Recovery

Most patients return to work and normal activities within 24–48 hours of surgery.

EVO ICL

Not a LASIK Candidate? EVO ICL.

EVO ICL (Implantable Collamer Lens) is a revolutionary lens implanted inside the eye — between the iris and the natural lens — to correct nearsightedness and astigmatism. Unlike LASIK, EVO ICL doesn’t remove any corneal tissue, making it ideal for patients who are not LASIK candidates. The EVO ICL is invisible inside the eye, requires no maintenance, and can be removed or replaced if your prescription changes significantly over time.

No Corneal Tissue Removed

Ideal for thin corneas, high prescriptions, or patients previously told they weren’t LASIK candidates.

UV Protection Built In

The Collamer material naturally filters UV light, providing built-in eye protection.

Reversible Procedure

Unlike LASIK, EVO ICL can be removed and replaced — providing flexibility as your vision needs evolve.

Outstanding Clarity

Patients consistently report exceptional visual quality, contrast, and night vision compared to their previous correction.

LASIK vs. EVO ICL — Which Is Right for You?

Both procedures deliver outstanding results. The right choice depends on your prescription, corneal thickness, and personal preferences. We’ll give you an honest recommendation based on your eyes — not a sales pitch.

LASIK

Custom Bladeless LASIK

  • Mild to moderate prescriptions
  • Adequate corneal thickness required
  • Stable Rx for 1–2 years
  • Fastest recovery — clear by next morning
  • Rx range: −8 to +6 D
  • Ages 18–50

EVO ICL

Implantable Collamer Lens

  • High prescriptions
  • Thin or irregular corneas
  • Reversible — can be removed or replaced
  • Safe for patients with dry eye
  • Rx range: up to −20 D
  • Thin corneas OK

LASIK & EVO ICL FAQ

LASIK is performed with numbing eye drops and is virtually painless during the procedure. Some patients experience mild discomfort, dryness, or light sensitivity in the first 24–48 hours after surgery, which typically resolves quickly.

LASIK results are permanent — the reshaping of the cornea does not reverse. However, as with all eyes, natural age-related changes (presbyopia) will still occur after age 40–45.

We recommend LASIK for patients 18 or older with a stable prescription for at least 1–2 years. There is no strict upper age limit, but patients over 45 should be aware of presbyopia.

Most EVO ICL patients return to work within 1–3 days. Vision stabilizes quickly. Contact sports and swimming should be avoided for about 2 weeks.
